## Admission Process Steps

1. **University Selection** - Identify suitable universities and programs
2. **Application Submission** - Submit application with required documents
3. **Receive Offer Letter** - Obtain conditional or unconditional admission offer
4. **Student Visa Application** - Initiate student visa process through employer/university
5. **Medical Test** - Undergo mandatory health screening
6. **Emirates ID** - Obtain UAE residence visa and Emirates ID
7. **Visa Approval** - Receive final visa clearance and documents
8. **Pre-Departure** - Arrange travel, accommodation, and health insurance
9. **University Enrollment** - Register and commence studies

## Key Information

### Study Visa Details
- **Duration**: Typically 2-4 years depending on program
- **Visa Type**: Student visa (residence visa)
- **Sponsorship**: University sponsors student visa
- **Work Authorization**: Generally not allowed during studies; some universities permit campus employment
- **Grace Period**: 30 days post-graduation before departure

### Cost Breakdown
- **Tuition**: AED 60,000 - 250,000/year (USD $16,000 - $68,000)
- **Living Expenses**: AED 24,000 - 48,000/year (USD $6,500 - $13,000)
- **Total Average**: AED 84,000 - 298,000/year (USD $23,000 - $81,000)

### Intake Periods
- **September (Fall)** - Main intake
- **January (Spring)** - Some programs
- **Summer (June)** - Limited programs

### English Language Requirements
- IELTS: 5.5 - 7.0 (depending on program)
- TOEFL: 70 - 100 (internet-based)
- Duolingo: 105 - 130
- University proficiency tests: Accepted at some institutions

### Work Opportunities
- **During Studies**: Limited, mostly university campus work
- **After Graduation**: Job market is highly competitive with excellent opportunities
- **Salary**: Tax-free income with competitive international salaries
- **Career Growth**: Strong opportunities in finance, tech, hospitality, and engineering
