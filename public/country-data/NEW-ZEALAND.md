## Admission Process Steps

1. **Register with Us** - Contact our office and register as a prospective student
2. **University Shortlisting** - Identify suitable universities based on your qualifications
3. **Application Submission** - Submit applications to shortlisted institutions
4. **Receive Offer Letter** - Obtain conditional or unconditional offer of admission
5. **Visa Application** - Apply for Student visa through New Zealand Immigration
6. **Visa Interview** - Attend visa interview if required
7. **Visa Approval** - Receive visa approval and documents
8. **Pre-Departure** - Arrange accommodation, travel, and health insurance
9. **University Enrollment** - Register and commence studies

## Key Information

### Study Visa Details
- **Duration**: Based on program length
- **Visa Type**: Student visa
- **Work Authorization**: 20 hours/week during studies, full-time during breaks
- **Post-Study Work Visa**: Available for most programs (typically 1-3 years)

### Cost Breakdown
- **Tuition**: NZD $25,000 - $45,000/year (varies by institution and program)
- **Living Expenses**: NZD $20,000 - $28,000/year
- **Total Average**: NZD $50,000 - $70,000/year

### Intake Periods
- **February (Semester 1)** - Main intake
- **July (Semester 2)** - Secondary intake
- **October/November (Semester 3)** - Some programs

### English Language Requirements
- IELTS: 6.0 - 6.5 (depending on program)
- TOEFL: 80 - 90 (internet-based)
- PTE: 50 - 58
- Cambridge ESOL: B2 - C1

### Work Opportunities
- On-campus employment: Widely available
- Off-campus work: 20 hours/week during semester, full-time during breaks
- Internships and co-op programs: Available across universities
- Post-graduation employment: Pathways to residency available

---

## Important Notes
- Processing time: 4-8 weeks for visa application
- Affordable living costs compared to Australia and UK
- Safe country with low crime rates
- Excellent work-life balance culture
- Strong pathway to permanent residency for skilled graduates
- Good employment market with competitive salaries
- Special visa pathways for skilled occupations
- Time zone advantages for connecting with Asian markets
